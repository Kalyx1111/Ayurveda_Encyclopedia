Extended Materia Medica — Additional Medicinal Herbs

Shatavari

Asparagus racemosus — शतावरी

Rasa: Sweet, bitter. Virya: Cooling. Female reproductive tonic, galactagogue, adaptogen. 100 roots = 100 husbands (strength metaphor). Builds Ojas, increases milk production, fertility tonic, anti-ulcer, immunomodulator.

Haritaki

Terminalia chebula — हरीतकी

Rasa: Five tastes (no sour). Virya: Heating. Called "Mother of herbs." Tridoshic. Digestive, laxative, anti-ageing, anti-bacterial, expectorant. Component of Triphala. Best herb for Vata. "Haritaki is like a mother."

Bibhitaki

Terminalia bellirica — विभीतकी

Rasa: Astringent. Virya: Heating. Primarily targets Kapha. Expectorant, anti-mucolytic, laxative, hair tonic, eye strengthener. Component of Triphala. Best herb for Kapha and respiratory conditions.

Manjistha

Rubia cordifolia — मंजिष्ठा

Rasa: Sweet, bitter, astringent. Virya: Heating. Best blood purifier in Ayurveda. Targets Pitta and Kapha in blood (Rakta dhatu). Skin diseases, lymphatic congestion, menstrual disorders, urinary tract, wound healing.

Vidari (Vidarikanda)

Pueraria tuberosa — विदारीकंद

Rasa: Sweet. Virya: Cooling. Excellent Vata and Pitta Rasayana. Muscle building, sexual vitality, milk production, strength, weight gain for underweight. Similar to wild yam but more Ayurvedic specific.

Bala (Country Mallow)

Sida cordifolia — बला

Rasa: Sweet. Virya: Cooling. Strongest nervine tonic in Ayurveda. Muscle strengthener, paralysis, post-stroke recovery, arthritis, MS. The root is used in Mahanarayan taila. Vata-specific Rasayana.

Punarnava

Boerhavia diffusa — पुनर्नवा

Rasa: Sweet, bitter. Virya: Cooling. Diuretic, anti-inflammatory, liver protective. Excellent for oedema, kidney disease, liver disorders, anaemia, obesity, Kapha disorders. Name means "renewer" — regenerates body tissues.

Shallaki (Boswellia)

Boswellia serrata — शल्लकी

Rasa: Bitter, astringent. Virya: Cooling. Most important anti-inflammatory herb. Arthritis, IBD, asthma, brain health (crosses blood-brain barrier). Strong clinical research evidence. Inhibits 5-LOX pathway.

Chitraka (Leadwort)

Plumbago zeylanica — चित्रक

Rasa: Pungent. Virya: Heating. Strongest Agni-kindler. Digestive fire stimulant, carminative, anti-parasitic, anti-obesity. Used in Chitrakaharitaki avaleha and many digestive formulations. Caution: Potent herb, use under supervision.

Kutki (Picrorhiza)

Picrorhiza kurroa — कुटकी

Rasa: Bitter. Virya: Cooling. Most powerful liver herb in Ayurveda. Hepatoprotective, choleretic, anti-inflammatory, anti-malarial, immune-modulator. Pitta and Kapha reducing. For hepatitis, liver cirrhosis, jaundice, skin diseases.

Jatamansi

Nardostachys jatamansi — जटामांसी

Rasa: Bitter, sweet, astringent. Virya: Cooling. Nervine sedative, anxiolytic, anti-convulsant, memory enhancer. Anxiety, epilepsy, palpitations, insomnia, hysteria, hair growth. Calms Vata and Pitta in the nervous system.

Vacha (Calamus)

Acorus calamus — वचा

Rasa: Bitter, pungent. Virya: Heating. Brain tonic, nervine stimulant, expectorant, anti-convulsant. Speech disorders, autism (research), epilepsy, memory, chronic sinusitis. Used in Brahmi preparations for synergy.

Dashamoola

Ten root combination — दशमूल

Composition: Bilva, Agnimantha, Shyonaka, Patala, Gambhari, Shalaparni, Prishniparni, Brihati, Kantakari, Gokshura. Tridoshic anti-inflammatory. Post-partum, respiratory, Vata disorders, fever, pain. Dashamoolaharitaki — classic formulation.

Shilajatu (Shilajit)

Asphaltum punjabianum — शिलाजतु

Not a plant — mineral resin from Himalayan rocks. Yoga (carrier) for all minerals. Anti-ageing, anti-diabetic, aphrodisiac, Ojas-building. "There is no disease in the world that Shilajatu cannot cure" — Charaka. Must be purified before use.

Gokshura

Tribulus terrestris — गोक्षुर

Rasa: Sweet. Virya: Cooling. Excellent diuretic, lithotriptic (dissolves kidney stones), reproductive tonic. UTI, kidney stones, sexual vitality, PCOS, prostate health. Balances Vata and Pitta in urinary system.

Pippali (Long Pepper)

Piper longum — पिप्पली

Rasa: Pungent. Virya: Heating. Respiratory herb, bioavailability enhancer (Yogavahi), digestive. Asthma, chronic cough, liver diseases. Key component of Trikatu. Pippali Rasayana — monthly increasing-decreasing dose protocol for longevity.

Kesar (Saffron)

Crocus sativus — केशर

Rasa: Sweet, bitter. Virya: Heating. Builds Ojas and Tejas. Anti-depressant (clinical RCT evidence), aphrodisiac, complexion enhancer, cardiac tonic. 2-4 strands in warm milk. Also used in Kesar-Ashwagandha milk for strength.

Arjuna

Terminalia arjuna — अर्जुन

Rasa: Astringent. Virya: Cooling. The supreme cardiac herb of Ayurveda. Heart failure, angina, myocardial protection, hypertension, hypercholesterolaemia. Take as Arjuna ksheerapaka (milk decoction). Clinical research strongly supports.

Nadi Pariksha — Pulse Diagnosis

Pulse diagnosis is considered one of the most sophisticated diagnostic tools in Ayurveda. The Vaidya (physician) uses three fingers (index, middle, ring) placed just below the wrist crease on the radial artery to assess the state of the doshas, dhatus, and organs. Classical texts describe the ability to diagnose diseases not yet manifested, past health history, and even future tendencies through pulse.

Finger Positions & Doshas

  • Index finger (Tarjani) — Vata pulse. Position: nearest the wrist crease. Character: Irregular, thin, fast, like a snake's movement (Sarpa gati)
  • Middle finger (Madhyama) — Pitta pulse. Character: Strong, sharp, jumping, like a frog (Manduka gati) or crow (Kaka gati)
  • Ring finger (Anamika) — Kapha pulse. Character: Slow, steady, deep, like a swan or pigeon (Hamsa/Kapota gati)

Pulse States

  • Sama Prakriti pulse: All three fingers feel balanced, regular, rhythmic
  • Vata excess: Index finger pulse dominant, irregular, feels like snake wriggling
  • Pitta excess: Middle finger dominant, jumping, forceful, warm
  • Kapha excess: Ring finger dominant, slow, cold, heavy, deep
  • Ama in pulse: Slow, deep, feels heavy and sticky — "coated" quality

Sapta Dhatu Nadi (Seven Tissue Pulses)

Advanced pulse reading can assess all seven tissues. The Vaidya presses at different depths:

  • Superficial (Tri-doshas): Vata (index), Pitta (middle), Kapha (ring)
  • Middle depth: Liver/Pitta organs (middle finger)
  • Deep depth: Dhatu assessment by rotating fingers

When to Take Pulse

  • Best: Early morning, before eating, on empty stomach
  • Not valid after: Heavy meal, exercise, bath, stress, alcohol
  • Most accurate: Brahma Muhurta (4-6am)
  • Both wrists: Left = Kapha-Pitta (left side organs), Right = Vata-Pitta (right side organs)

Marma Points — Vital Energy Points

Marma points are 107 vital energy points described in Sushruta Samhita — junctions of muscles, veins, ligaments, bones, and joints where life force (Prana) is concentrated. They are the Ayurvedic equivalent of acupuncture points and chakra intersections. Injury to major Marma points can be life-threatening; gentle stimulation heals and energises.

Classification by Tissue (Mamsala, etc.)

  • Mamsa Marma (11): Situated in muscle tissue. Example: Talahridaya (center of palm)
  • Sira Marma (41): Situated in blood vessels/veins. Example: Stanamula (breast root)
  • Snayu Marma (27): Situated in tendons/ligaments. Example: Ani (above knee)
  • Asthi Marma (8): Situated in bone. Example: Katikataruna (hip bone)
  • Sandhi Marma (20): Situated in joints. Example: Kurpara (elbow joint)

Classification by Effect if Injured

  • Sadya Pranahara (3): Immediately fatal — Brahmarandhra (crown), Hridaya (heart), Shringataka (throat)
  • Kalantara Pranahara (33): Fatal over time if untreated
  • Vishalyaghna (3): Fatal only on removal of weapon
  • Vaikalyakara (44): Causes deformity/disability
  • Rujakara (22): Causes severe pain

Important Therapeutic Marma Points

Adhipati — Crown of Head

Location: Top of skull, Brahmarandhra

Governs: Prana, consciousness, pineal gland. Gentle stimulation with Brahmi oil: clarity, spiritual awareness, headache relief.

Sthapani — Third Eye

Location: Between eyebrows, Ajna chakra

Governs: Mind, pituitary, intuition. Shirodhara oil falls here. Stimulation relieves headaches, anxiety, eye strain, sinus congestion.

Hridaya — Heart Point

Location: Centre of sternum

Governs: Heart, emotional centre, Prana Vata. Gentle pressure with warm ghee relieves palpitations, grief, emotional pain.

Nabhi — Navel Point

Location: Navel centre

Governs: Agni, Samana Vata, small intestine. Filling navel with warm sesame oil overnight balances Vata, improves digestion, relieves dryness.

Kshipra — Between Thumb and Index

Location: Webbing between thumb and index finger (hand and foot)

Governs: Prana flow, emergency resuscitation point. Pressure here revives consciousness in fainting.

Talahridaya — Palm and Sole Centre

Location: Centre of palm; centre of sole

Governs: Entire body through reflex connections. Foot Abhyanga (oil massage of soles) with sesame oil before sleep promotes deep sleep, grounds Vata, improves vision.

Rasa (Taste) Science — Shad Rasa

Manasa Roga — Mental Health in Ayurveda

Ayurveda considers the mind (Manas) as governed by three Gunas — Sattva (purity, clarity), Rajas (activity, passion), and Tamas (inertia, heaviness). Mental disease arises from the imbalance of these gunas combined with doshic involvement.

Three Manasika Doshas

  • Sattva (Pure): Clarity, compassion, wisdom, equanimity. Promotes health of mind and spirit. Goal of all Ayurvedic mental treatment is to increase Sattva.
  • Rajas (Active): Desire, anger, ambition, restlessness. Causes anxiety, aggression, manipulation. Pitta-related.
  • Tamas (Inert): Heaviness, delusion, attachment, depression, confusion, sleep. Kapha-related.

Sattvavajaya — Mental Discipline

  • Control of mind through knowledge (Jnana), wisdom (Vijnana), and courage (Dhairya)
  • Memory training (Smriti) — daily practice of memorising sacred texts
  • Concentration (Samadhi) — meditation practice
  • Proper use of sense organs — Indriya Vigyana
  • Satsanga — association with wise, noble, uplifting people
  • Mantra chanting — Vibration therapy for the mind

Sattvic Foods for Mental Health

  • Fresh organic fruits and vegetables
  • Pure cow's milk (warm, spiced)
  • Ghee — called "brain food" by Ayurveda
  • Honey (raw, unheated)
  • Almonds, walnuts (soaked)
  • Sesame seeds and tahini
  • Basmati rice and whole grains
  • Mung dal (easiest to digest)
  • Fresh herbs: Coriander, mint, tulsi

Rajasic / Tamasic Foods to Minimise

  • Tamasic: Meat, fish, eggs, alcohol, stale/leftover food, onion (in excess), garlic (in excess), mushrooms, overeating
  • Rajasic: Excess spicy food, coffee, tea, excessive salt, stimulants, too much sugar, fast food

Agni & Ama — Digestive Fire & Metabolic Toxins

The 13 Types of Agni

  • Jatharagni (1): Primary gastric fire in stomach/small intestine. King of all Agnis. Controls all other Agnis.
  • Dhatvagni (7): One in each of the seven dhatus — processes raw material into each tissue type
  • Bhutagni (5): One for each of the five elements — processes element-specific components of food

Signs of Sama Agni (Balanced)

  • Good appetite but not excessive
  • Complete digestion with no bloating/gas
  • Normal stool — brown, floats, no odour
  • Light body, clear mind after eating
  • Good immunity and tissue quality

Signs of Ama (Metabolic Toxins)

  • White coating on tongue (most reliable sign)
  • Foul breath and body odour
  • Heaviness after eating, fatigue
  • Blocked channels — congestion, constipation
  • Dull, cloudy feeling in mind
  • Pulse: Deep, slow, feels sticky or coated
  • Loss of taste and appetite

Ama-Removing Protocol

  • Langhan (fasting/lightening) — most powerful Ama remover
  • Trikatu — burns Ama at source
  • Warm water throughout day — flushes Ama
  • Ginger tea — digestive and Ama-clearing
  • Triphala at night — clears intestinal Ama
  • Avoid heavy meals — give Agni rest to clear backlog

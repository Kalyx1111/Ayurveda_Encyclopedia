Foundations

Introduction to Ayurveda

आयुर्वेद — Ayurveda means "The Science of Life" (Ayur = life, Veda = knowledge/science). It is the world's oldest holistic healing system, originating in ancient India over 5,000 years ago.

Origins & History

Ayurveda traces its origins to the Atharva Veda, one of the four sacred Vedas of ancient India. According to tradition, the knowledge was revealed by Brahma to Daksha Prajapati, then passed to the Ashwins, Indra, and eventually to the sages Punarvasu Atreya and Dhanvantari who systematised it into the medical system we know today.

The two primary classical texts are the Charaka Samhita (internal medicine, compiled ~300 BCE), and the Sushruta Samhita (surgery, ~600 BCE). The Ashtanga Hridayam by Vagbhata (~7th century CE) synthesised both schools. Together these are called the Brihatrayi — the Great Trio.

Eight Branches (Ashtanga Ayurveda)

  • Kaya Chikitsa — Internal medicine, treatment of the whole body
  • Bala Chikitsa (Kaumarabhritya) — Paediatrics and obstetrics
  • Graha Chikitsa — Psychiatry, mental health, possession disorders
  • Shalakya Tantra — ENT, ophthalmology, dentistry (above the neck)
  • Shalya Tantra — Surgery (Sushruta performed rhinoplasty 2600 years ago)
  • Agada Tantra — Toxicology, antidotes to poisons
  • Rasayana Tantra — Rejuvenation, anti-ageing, immunology
  • Vajikarana Tantra — Aphrodisiology, reproductive medicine

Core Philosophical Foundations

Panchamahabhuta — Five Great Elements

All matter in the universe, including the human body, is composed of five fundamental elements:

  • Akasha (Space/Ether) — The space within and between all things; governs sound and communication
  • Vayu (Air) — Movement, breath, nervous system, all motion in the body
  • Agni (Fire) — Transformation, digestion, metabolism, intelligence, vision
  • Jala (Water) — Fluidity, cohesion, plasma, blood, mucus, all body fluids
  • Prithvi (Earth) — Solidity, structure, bones, muscles, teeth, nails, skin

Tridosha Theory

The Five Elements combine to form three biological forces — Vata (Air + Space), Pitta (Fire + Water), and Kapha (Earth + Water) — known as the Tridoshas. These govern all physiological and psychological functions. Disease arises when doshas are imbalanced; health is the state of their harmonious equilibrium.

Sapta Dhatu — Seven Body Tissues

  • Rasa (Plasma, lymph) — Nourishment, first tissue formed from digested food
  • Rakta (Blood) — Oxygenation, life force, colour
  • Mamsa (Muscle tissue) — Movement, body shape, strength
  • Meda (Adipose/Fat tissue) — Lubrication, insulation, energy reserve
  • Asthi (Bone tissue) — Structure, support, protection
  • Majja (Bone marrow, nervous tissue) — Filling of bones, neural function
  • Shukra/Artava (Reproductive tissue) — Procreation, vitality, immunity

Agni — Digestive Fire

Agni (metabolic fire) is arguably the most important concept in Ayurvedic medicine. The primary form is Jatharagni (gastric fire). Balanced Agni (Sama Agni) produces healthy tissues. There are four states: Sama (balanced), Vishama (irregular — Vata), Tikshna (sharp — Pitta), and Manda (slow — Kapha). Most Ayurvedic treatment aims at correcting Agni first.

Ama — Metabolic Toxins

When Agni is impaired, incompletely digested food creates Ama — a sticky, toxic substance that clogs the channels (Srotas) and is the root cause of most disease according to Ayurveda.

Classical Texts of Ayurveda

  • Charaka Samhita — ~300 BCE. 8 Sthanas, 120 chapters. Internal medicine focus. Most authoritative text on Kayachikitsa.
  • Sushruta Samhita — ~600 BCE. Surgical techniques, anatomy, 1120 diseases, 700 medicinal plants, 121 surgical instruments.
  • Ashtanga Hridayam — Vagbhata, 7th century CE. Synthesis of Charaka and Sushruta schools.
  • Ashtanga Sangraha — Also by Vagbhata. More elaborate version of Ashtanga Hridayam.
  • Madhava Nidana — 7th–9th century CE. Definitive work on diagnosis and pathology.
  • Sharangadhara Samhita — 13th–14th century CE. Pharmaceutical preparations, pulse diagnosis, formulations.
  • Bhavaprakasha — 16th century CE. Materia medica, 500+ herbs, food items, minerals.
  • Dhanvantari Nighantu — Classical lexicon of medicinal substances.
  • Raja Nighantu — Comprehensive drug dictionary with synonyms and properties.
  • Kashyapa Samhita — Paediatrics, obstetrics, gynaecology.

The Three Doshas

Vata, Pitta and Kapha are the three bio-energetic forces derived from the Panchamahabhutas. They govern every physiological, psychological and emotional function in the human body.

VATA
वात — Wind & Space
🌬 Air 🌌 Space

Qualities: Dry, light, cold, rough, subtle, mobile, clear. Governs all movement in the body — breathing, circulation, nerve impulses, elimination. Seat: Colon, pelvic region, bones, skin, ears, thighs.

PITTA
पित्त — Fire & Water
🔥 Fire 💧 Water

Qualities: Hot, sharp, light, liquid, oily, spreading, malodorous. Governs transformation — digestion, metabolism, intelligence, vision, skin colour, body temperature. Seat: Small intestine, stomach, liver, spleen, blood, skin, eyes.

KAPHA
कफ — Earth & Water
🌍 Earth 💧 Water

Qualities: Heavy, slow, cold, oily, smooth, dense, soft, stable, cloudy, sticky. Governs structure and lubrication — immunity, growth, strength, memory, love. Seat: Chest, lungs, throat, head, sinuses, mouth, stomach, joints.

Vata Dosha — Detailed

Five Sub-Doshas of Vata

  • Prana Vata — Governs: Breath, swallowing, sneezing, spitting, belching. Located in: Head, chest. Disorders: Anxiety, breathlessness, hiccup, neurological conditions.
  • Udana Vata — Governs: Upward movement, speech, effort, memory, complexion. Located in: Throat, chest, navel. Disorders: Speech disorders, thyroid, vertigo.
  • Samana Vata — Governs: Digestive movements, absorption. Located in: Small intestine. Disorders: Malabsorption, digestive issues.
  • Apana Vata — Governs: Downward elimination — urination, defaecation, menstruation, childbirth. Located in: Colon, pelvis. Disorders: Constipation, menstrual irregularity, prostate issues.
  • Vyana Vata — Governs: Circulation, heart function, perspiration, movement throughout body. Located in: Heart, pervades whole body. Disorders: Circulation problems, hypertension.

Vata Imbalance Signs

  • Physical: Dry skin, cracking joints, constipation, weight loss, cold extremities, insomnia, irregular appetite, gas, bloating
  • Mental: Anxiety, fear, nervousness, indecisiveness, scattered thinking, memory problems, restlessness

Vata Balancing

  • Diet: Warm, oily, heavy, sweet, sour, salty foods. Ghee, sesame oil, warm milk, root vegetables, rice, wheat, nuts
  • Herbs: Ashwagandha, Shatavari, Bala, Vidari, Dashamoola, Licorice, Ginger
  • Lifestyle: Regular warm oil massage (Abhyanga), warmth, regular routine, rest, meditation
  • Avoid: Cold, dry, raw, light foods; excessive travel, screen time, irregular sleep

Pitta Dosha — Detailed

Five Sub-Doshas of Pitta

  • Pachaka Pitta — Primary digestive fire in the small intestine. Digests and transforms food. Root of all other Pittas.
  • Ranjaka Pitta — Located in liver, spleen, stomach. Gives colour to blood, bile, stool.
  • Sadhaka Pitta — Located in heart. Enables achievement of goals, intelligence, memory, emotions.
  • Alochaka Pitta — Located in eyes. Enables vision and processing of visual information.
  • Bhrajaka Pitta — Located in skin. Gives lustre, colour; processes substances applied topically.

Pitta Imbalance Signs

  • Physical: Acidity, acid reflux, ulcers, inflammation, skin rashes, acne, excessive hunger, diarrhoea, fever, burning sensations, premature greying, hair loss
  • Mental: Anger, irritability, criticism, perfectionism, competitiveness, jealousy, impatience

Pitta Balancing

  • Diet: Cool, sweet, bitter, astringent foods. Coconut, milk, ghee, sweet fruits, green vegetables, coriander, mint, fennel
  • Herbs: Shatavari, Amalaki, Guduchi, Brahmi, Neem, Sandalwood, Aloe vera, Turmeric
  • Lifestyle: Cooling activities, moonlight walks, swimming, creative work, rest, minimise conflict
  • Avoid: Spicy, hot, sour, oily foods; alcohol, excessive sun, competitive pressure

Kapha Dosha — Detailed

Five Sub-Doshas of Kapha

  • Kledaka Kapha — Located in stomach. Moistens food, protects stomach lining, initiates digestion.
  • Avalambaka Kapha — Located in heart and chest. Supports heart function, provides strength to back and chest.
  • Bodhaka Kapha — Located in mouth and tongue. Enables taste perception, lubricates mouth.
  • Tarpaka Kapha — Located in head, sinuses. Nourishes sense organs, keeps them moist.
  • Sleshaka Kapha — Located in joints. Lubricates all joints, maintains their integrity.

Kapha Imbalance Signs

  • Physical: Weight gain, water retention, congestion, mucus overproduction, slow digestion, lethargy, excessive sleep, cold, cough, diabetes, high cholesterol
  • Mental: Attachment, depression, greed, possessiveness, stubbornness, excessive sentimentality, mental fog

Kapha Balancing

  • Diet: Light, dry, warm, spicy, bitter, pungent foods. Ginger, black pepper, honey (not heated), legumes, bitter greens, millet, barley
  • Herbs: Trikatu (three peppers), Punarnava, Guggulu, Bibhitaki, Chitrak, Vacha, Kutki
  • Lifestyle: Vigorous exercise, dry massage (Udvartana), early rising, fasting, stimulating activities
  • Avoid: Heavy, sweet, oily, cold, dairy-rich foods; oversleeping, daytime naps, sedentary lifestyle

Ahara Vidhi

Diet, Food & Nutrition

In Ayurveda, food is medicine — Ahara is the first of the three pillars of life (Traya Upastambha), along with Sleep and Brahmacharya. What you eat, when you eat, how you eat, and how you combine foods determines your health.

Ahara Vidhi Visheshayatana — Rules of Eating

  • Ushnam Ashniyat — Always eat warm food. Cold food diminishes Agni and creates Ama.
  • Snigdham Ashniyat — Eat food with sufficient fat/oil. Dry food disturbs Vata.
  • Matravat Ashniyat — Eat the right quantity — fill half stomach with food, quarter with water, leave quarter empty for digestion.
  • Jirneashniyat — Eat only when the previous meal is digested (approximately 4-6 hours).
  • Aviruddham Ashniyat — Do not eat incompatible food combinations (Viruddha Ahara).
  • Ishtadeshe — Eat in a pleasant, comfortable environment.
  • Na Atidrutam — Do not eat too fast — chew thoroughly.
  • Na Ati Vilambitam — Do not eat too slowly — food becomes cold and digestion is impaired.
  • Tanmanabhunjita — Eat with full attention on food. No distractions, screens, or arguments while eating.

Viruddha Ahara — Incompatible Food Combinations

According to Charaka Samhita, combining these foods creates toxins (Ama) in the body:

  • Milk + Fish — Most serious incompatibility. Causes skin diseases, vitiligo.
  • Milk + Sour fruits — Causes bloating, indigestion, skin disorders.
  • Honey + Ghee in equal proportions — Toxic combination (use in different amounts is fine).
  • Honey + Warm water/food — Heating honey above 40°C makes it toxic; use only at room temperature or below.
  • Milk + Salt — Causes skin diseases.
  • Curd + Milk — Both are heavy; together they ferment and create Ama.
  • Fruit + Milk — Except ripe sweet mango. Most fruits are incompatible with milk.
  • Cold water after hot foods — Disrupts digestion and Agni.
  • Radish + Milk, banana, raisins, black gram — Various incompatibilities documented.

Dinacharya

Daily Routine — Dinacharya

Charaka Samhita states: "A person who follows Dinacharya attains longevity, health, intelligence, fame, complexion, strength, and all living beings love him." The daily routine aligns human biology with the rhythms of nature.

4:00 – 6:00 AM — Brahma Muhurta
Wake Up — Brahma Muhurta
Awaken 96 minutes before sunrise — the "Creator's Hour." The atmosphere is rich in prana, the mind is fresh and calm. This is ideal for meditation, study, and spiritual practice. Lie quietly for a moment and express gratitude. Avoid waking in Kapha time (6–10am) as it causes heaviness and lethargy.
5:30 AM
Ushapana — Water Therapy
Drink 2-4 cups of warm water (copper vessel water stored overnight is ideal) on an empty stomach. This flushes the kidneys, stimulates bowel movement, hydrates cells, and activates digestion. Classical texts recommend drinking until you feel the urge to eliminate.
6:00 AM
Mala Visarjana — Elimination
Natural elimination of bowel and bladder. Ideally happens naturally without straining. Constipation is the root of many diseases in Ayurveda. Haritaki, Triphala, or warm water with ghee can assist. Never suppress natural urges — this is a cardinal rule in Ayurveda.
6:15 AM
Danta Dhavana — Oral Hygiene
Brush teeth with herbal twigs (Neem, Licorice, Karanja) or herbal tooth powder. Follow with Jihva Nirlekhana (tongue scraping) with a copper or stainless steel scraper — removes Ama, activates digestive organs through tongue-organ connections. Then Kavala or Gandusha — oil pulling with sesame or coconut oil (5-20 minutes).
6:30 AM
Nasya — Nasal Oiling
Apply 2-3 drops of warm sesame oil or Anu taila in each nostril. The nose is the gateway to the head and brain. Daily Nasya lubricates nasal passages, improves voice, vision and clarity, and prevents sinus problems. "The nose is the door to the head" — Ashtanga Hridayam.
6:45 AM
Abhyanga — Self-Oil Massage
Warm oil self-massage of the entire body for 10-20 minutes before bathing. "Abhyanga should be resorted to daily. It wards off old age, exertion and Vata disorders. It improves eyesight, nourishes the body, increases longevity, induces sleep, and strengthens the skin." — Ashtanga Hridayam. Use sesame oil (Vata), coconut oil (Pitta), or mustard oil (Kapha).
7:15 AM
Vyayama — Exercise
Exercise to half capacity (Ardhashakti) — until you can no longer breathe through the nose easily. Best time is Vata/Kapha period (6-10am). Yoga, walking, swimming are most universally recommended. Vata types: gentle exercise. Pitta: cooling exercise. Kapha: vigorous exercise. Exercise should be adapted to age, season, and constitution.
8:00 AM
Snana — Bathing
Warm water bath (avoid cold shower for Vata types and in winter). Bath purifies the body, removes Abhyanga oil, increases Agni, clears fatigue, and refreshes the mind. Use herbal powders (chickpea flour, neem powder) instead of harsh chemical soaps where possible. Always wash head separately — do not use hot water on the head.
8:30 AM
Breakfast — Prata Ahara
Warm, light breakfast. Ideal: warm porridge, cooked grains, warm spiced milk, fresh fruit (eaten alone, not combined). Avoid cold cereal, fruit juices, smoothies (cold and raw — hard to digest). "Do not eat breakfast if not hungry" — the digestive fire from the previous night should be fully active first.
12:00 – 1:00 PM
Lunch — Madhyahna Ahara (Main Meal)
Largest meal of the day. Pitta time (10am-2pm) = digestive fire at peak. Sit down, eat warm freshly cooked food. Include all six tastes (sweet, sour, salty, pungent, bitter, astringent). Eat to 3/4 fullness. Drink warm water or warm spiced water (jeera/fennel) during meal. Rest for 10 minutes after eating — Vajrasana (thunderbolt pose) aids digestion.
2:00 – 6:00 PM
Afternoon Work & Rest
Productive work time. A short walk after lunch is beneficial. Avoid sleeping in the afternoon (except in summer or for elderly, children, debilitated). If tired, a 10-minute Shavasana or seated rest without sleep. Afternoon is ideal for creative work, learning, and physical activity for Pitta types.
6:00 – 7:00 PM
Evening Practices
Light walking or gentle yoga. Evening prayer or meditation. This is Vata time — creativity, reflection, spiritual practice. Avoid heavy exercise after sunset. A warm cup of herbal tea (ginger, cardamom, tulsi) is ideal. Review the day with gratitude.
7:00 – 8:00 PM
Dinner — Sayam Ahara (Light)
Light, warm, easily digestible dinner — eaten at least 3 hours before sleep. Soups, khichadi, light grains. Avoid heavy proteins, dairy (except warm milk), raw salads at night. "One who eats a light dinner is healthy, sleeps well, and lives long." — Charaka.
9:30 PM
Warm Milk (Ksheerapana)
Warm golden milk (with Ashwagandha, turmeric, cardamom, saffron) 30 minutes before sleep. Builds Ojas, promotes deep sleep, calms Vata. Gently massage the soles of feet with warm sesame oil before sleep for grounding and to promote sound sleep.
10:00 PM
Nidra — Sleep
Sleep by 10pm — before the Pitta period (10pm-2am). Sleeping during Pitta time causes the body to process and rejuvenate. Missing this window by staying awake past 11pm means the mind becomes active again (Pitta energy) and sleep quality suffers. Sleep on left side for better digestion. Avoid screens 1 hour before sleep.

Ayurvedic Treatments

Ayurvedic treatment works on two main principles: Shodhana (purification/elimination) and Shamana (pacification/palliative). Treatment always addresses the root cause (Nidana), not just symptoms.

Bahya Snehana · External Oleation
Systematic warm oil massage using medicated oils appropriate to the patient's constitution. The therapist uses specific strokes that follow the direction of body hair and lymphatic flow. Duration: 45-60 minutes. Oils used include Bala Ashwagandha tailam (Vata), Brahmi coconut oil (Pitta), Mustard or Karpura tailam (Kapha). Shodana Chikitsa

Panchakarma

Panchakarma means "Five Actions" — the five classical bio-purification procedures that eliminate accumulated toxins from the deep tissues. It is the most powerful and complete detoxification system in Ayurveda. Always preceded by Purvakarma (preparatory procedures).

⚠️
Panchakarma procedures must ONLY be performed under the direct supervision of a qualified Ayurvedic physician. Improper administration can be dangerous. This information is for educational understanding only.

Purvakarma — Preparatory Procedures

Before the main Panchakarma procedures, the body must be prepared over 3-7 days to liquefy and mobilise the doshas from their sites of accumulation into the digestive tract for elimination.

Snehana — Oleation Therapy

Internal: Increasing doses of medicated ghee taken on empty stomach for 3-7 days (Antara Snehana). External: Full body oil massage (Abhyanga). Lubricates the channels, loosens toxins from tissue, makes doshas mobile.

Swedana — Sudation/Sweating Therapy

After oleation, heat is applied to the body through steam box (Bashpa Sweda), herbal boluses, or hot water immersion. Heat opens channels, dilates vessels, mobilises loosened toxins further toward the digestive system.

The Five Panchakarma Procedures

1. Vamana — Therapeutic Emesis

Medicated vomiting to eliminate excess Kapha from the stomach and chest. Indications: Bronchial asthma, chronic sinusitis, psoriasis, diabetes, obesity, epilepsy, repeated tonsillitis. Medicines used: Licorice + honey + Vacha, Madanaphala, Neem decoction. CONTRAINDICATED: Elderly, children under 12, pregnancy, cardiac patients, severe debility.

2. Virechana — Therapeutic Purgation

Medicated laxative therapy to eliminate excess Pitta from the small intestine and liver. The most frequently performed Panchakarma procedure — safe, effective, and indicated for most Pitta conditions. Medicines: Castor oil (Erandataila), Trivrit (Operculina turpethum), Senna, Trivritti avaleha. Indications: Skin diseases, acne, psoriasis, eczema, liver disorders, hyperacidity, anaemia, headaches, infertility.

3. Basti — Medicated Enema Therapy

Considered the most important of the five procedures — called "Ardha Chikitsa" (half of all treatment) by Charaka. Medicated oils, ghee, or herbal decoctions are administered through the rectum. Two types: Anuvasana Basti (oil enema, retained) and Niruha/Asthapana Basti (decoction enema, expelled). Indications: Constipation, IBS, Vata disorders, arthritis, neurological conditions, infertility, osteoporosis. The colon is the primary seat of Vata — Basti directly pacifies all Vata disorders.

4. Nasya — Nasal Administration

Medicated substances administered through the nostrils to cleanse the head region (above the neck). Eliminates excess Kapha and Vata from the head and neck. Indications: Migraine, sinusitis, brain disorders, eye disorders, hair loss, premature greying, Bell's palsy, cervical spondylosis. The most important procedure for all conditions affecting the head.

5. Raktamokshana — Bloodletting

Blood purification therapy. Methods: Jalauka (leech therapy — most commonly used today), Pracchana (scarification), Siravyadha (venesection), Shringa (horn suction). Removes vitiated Rakta (blood) carrying Pitta toxins. Indications: Skin diseases, gout, varicose veins, abscess, inflammatory joint conditions, herpes. Jalauka (leech) therapy is experiencing a significant revival for localised conditions.

Paschat Karma — Post-Panchakarma Care

After purification, the body channels are clean and the digestive fire is temporarily reduced. Strict dietary protocols (Samsarjana Krama) must be followed for 3-7 days: beginning with warm water and rice gruel (peya), progressing to thicker rice gruel (vilepi), then kichadi, then normal diet. Lifestyle is restricted — no exertion, exposure to extremes of weather, sexual activity, or emotional stress. Rasayana therapy begins after Panchakarma for maximum benefit.

Rejuvenation Science

Rasayana — The Science of Rejuvenation

Rasayana is the eighth branch of Ayurveda dedicated to rejuvenation, longevity, and the enhancement of all body tissues. "Rasa" means essence/the first tissue (plasma), "Ayana" means path — Rasayana is what promotes the ideal flow of nourishment through all seven tissues to ultimately produce Ojas.

Major Rasayana Preparations

Chyawanprash

The most famous Rasayana. Classical formula from Charaka Samhita. Credited to sage Chyavana who was rejuvenated by it. Contains ~35-50 ingredients including Amalaki (base — 25%), Ashwagandha, Shatavari, Bala, Brahmi, Pippali, Guduchi, Vidarikanda, ghee, honey, sesame oil, and many other herbs. Benefits: Immune enhancement, anti-ageing, lung health, reproductive vitality, strength, Ojas building. Dose: 1-2 tsp twice daily, with warm milk.

Brahma Rasayana

Superior brain Rasayana from Charaka Samhita. 49 ingredients base of Amalaki with 49 herbs. Specifically for enhancing memory, intelligence, longevity, and sensory acuity.

Ashwagandha Rasayana

Ashwagandha powder mixed with ghee, honey, and warm milk. Builds muscle, strength, sexual vitality, and emotional resilience. Particularly indicated for Vata types and those suffering from debility, anxiety, or fatigue.

Amalaki Rasayana

Fresh or dried Amalaki prepared with honey and ghee. Charaka states it is the best of all Rasayanas — anti-ageing, balances all three doshas, promotes all seven dhatus, enhances intelligence and strength.

Shatavari Rasayana

Shatavari + ghee + milk + honey. Supreme Rasayana for women — promotes fertility, milk production, hormonal balance, and nourishment. Also beneficial for men for reproductive vitality.

Ojas — The Essence of Vitality

Ojas is the ultimate refined essence produced from the perfect transformation of all seven body tissues. It is the physical substrate of immunity, vitality, spiritual radiance, and longevity. "Ojas is the essence of all Dhatus" — Charaka. Located primarily in the heart.

Foods that build Ojas

  • Organic cow's milk (warm, with spices) — supreme Ojas-builder
  • Pure ghee (clarified butter from cow's milk)
  • Natural honey (raw, unheated)
  • Almonds (soaked and peeled)
  • Saffron in warm milk
  • Dates and figs (soaked)
  • Coconut — water, meat, and oil
  • Amalaki (fresh or as Chyawanprash)
  • Sesame seeds and tahini
  • Organic rice and wheat

What depletes Ojas

  • Excessive physical exertion beyond one's capacity
  • Excessive sexual activity
  • Fasting beyond capacity, skipping meals
  • Chronic stress, worry, grief, fear
  • Sleep deprivation — most depleting of all
  • Excessive talking, anger, overwork
  • Alcohol, drugs, junk food
  • Chronic illness
Bhaishajya Kalpana

Classical Formulations

Ayurvedic pharmacy (Bhaishajya Kalpana) classifies medicines into five primary and numerous secondary preparation forms. There are over 2,000 classical formulations documented across the major texts.

Dosage Forms (Kalpana)

  • Churna (Powder) — Fine herbal powder. Examples: Triphala churna, Trikatu churna, Ashwagandha churna, Avipattikar churna
  • Kwatha/Kashaya (Decoction) — 1 part herb to 16 parts water, boiled down to 1/4. Examples: Dashamoola kashaya, Panchatikta kashaya, Punarnavadi kashaya
  • Avaleha/Lehya (Linctus/Jam) — Semi-solid preparation. Examples: Chyawanprash, Vasavaleha, Agastya haritaki
  • Taila (Medicated Oil) — Herbs cooked in base oil. Examples: Mahanarayan taila, Ksheerabala taila, Brahmi taila, Anu taila
  • Ghrita (Medicated Ghee) — Herbs processed in ghee. Examples: Brahmi ghrita, Triphala ghrita, Mahatiktak ghrita
  • Vati/Gutika (Tablet/Pill) — Compressed herbal pills. Examples: Arogyavardhini vati, Chandraprabha vati, Triphala guggulu
  • Bhasma (Calcined ash) — Metals/minerals purified through extensive processing. Examples: Swarna (gold) bhasma, Tamra (copper) bhasma, Abhrak bhasma
  • Arishta/Asava (Fermented preparations) — Self-generated alcohol extracts. Examples: Ashwagandharishta, Dashmularishta, Kutajarishta, Drakshasava
  • Arka (Distillate) — Steam distillate of herbs. Examples: Ajwain arka, Pudina arka
  • Rasakriya (Thick extract) — Evaporated decoction. Examples: Satwa of Guduchi, Giloy satwa

Important Classical Formulations

Arogyavardhini Vati

Contains Triphala, Shilajatu, Guggulu, Chitrak, Kutki, Pippali, Neem. Indications: Liver diseases, obesity, skin diseases, fever, ascites. One of the most versatile classical formulations.

Mahanarayan Taila

Complex medicated oil with 55+ ingredients including Shatavari, Bala, Ashwagandha, Dashamoola in sesame oil base. Indications: Arthritis, muscle weakness, sports injuries, post-fracture recovery, all Vata disorders. Widely used for Abhyanga and Basti.

Chandraprabha Vati

Contains Shilajatu, Guggulu, Triphala, Trikatu, Vacha, Mustha, and other herbs. Indications: Urinary tract infections, kidney stones, prostate disorders, diabetes, sexual weakness, general debility.

Ashwagandharishta

Fermented preparation with Ashwagandha as the main herb. Indications: Anxiety, stress, insomnia, debility, mental fatigue, post-illness weakness, low libido. Self-generated alcohol acts as carrier to enhance absorption.

Kanchanar Guggulu

Contains Kanchanara bark, Triphala, Trikatu, Varun, Tejpatra, Ela, Tvak, Guggulu. Indications: Thyroid disorders (both hypo and hyper), lymphatic congestion, cysts, lipomas, PCOS, swollen glands.

Hingvastak Churna

Contains Asafoetida (Hing), Trikatu (three peppers), Ajwain, Saindhava, Sunthi. Indications: All types of digestive complaints — gas, bloating, constipation, colic, poor appetite, irritable bowel. Taken with ghee before meals.

Ritucharya

Seasonal Routine — Ritucharya

Ayurveda recognises six seasons (in the Indian subcontinent) and prescribes specific diet, lifestyle, and health practices for each. Following Ritucharya prevents seasonal diseases and maintains doshic balance throughout the year.

🌸

Vasanta (Spring) — Feb-Apr

Kapha that accumulated in winter melts and can cause congestion, allergies, lethargy. This is Kapha season. Ideal time for Vamana (therapeutic emesis). Diet: Light, dry, bitter foods. Barley, ginger, honey water, bitter vegetables. Exercise vigorously. Avoid: Sweets, dairy, cold foods, daytime sleep.

☀️

Grishma (Summer) — May-Jul

Pitta increases. Heat depletes strength. Body needs cooling and hydration. Diet: Sweet, liquid, cold (but not ice-cold), light foods. Coconut water, sweet fruits, milk, rice, sugar cane juice. Limit salt and spicy food. Bathe in cool water. Rest during midday. Moonlight walks beneficial.

🌧️

Varsha (Monsoon) — Jul-Sep

Vata increases due to irregular digestion from humidity. Agni is weakest. Best time for Basti. Diet: Light, easily digestible — rice, wheat, old grains, ghee, ginger. Medicated wine (Arishtasava) in small amounts with food. Boil all drinking water. Avoid: Raw foods, heavy meals, daytime sleep, excess exercise.

🍂

Sharad (Autumn) — Sep-Nov

Pitta accumulated in summer erupts. Ideal time for Virechana (purgation). Diet: Sweet, bitter, astringent, light, dry. Wheat, rice, green gram, barley, honey. Amla is excellent. Avoid: Curd, fish, sesame, meat, alcohol, exposure to direct sun. Moonlight is beneficial — moon baths for Pitta.

❄️

Hemanta (Early Winter) — Nov-Jan

Agni is strongest — best digestive capacity. Strength and vitality are at peak. Build Ojas. Diet: Heavy, nourishing, all tastes. Milk, ghee, meat (if non-vegetarian), urad dal, sesame, wine in moderation. Vigorous exercise, oil massage. Sexual activity permissible. Avoid: Cold, light, dry foods.

🌨️

Shishira (Late Winter) — Jan-Feb

Similar to Hemanta but transitioning. Kapha begins to accumulate. Diet: Similar to Hemanta but start introducing lighter foods as Kapha builds. Warm, oily, nourishing but not as heavy. Begin increasing activity to prevent Kapha stagnation. Maintain oil massage and warm baths daily.

Roga Nidana

Conditions & Ayurvedic Approach

Ayurveda describes over 1,120 diseases. Disease (Vyadhi) is the result of the imbalance of Doshas disturbing the normal functioning of the Dhatus (tissues), Agni (digestive fire), Malas (waste products), and the Srotas (channels).

⚠️
Information below is for research and educational purposes. All Ayurvedic treatment protocols must be prescribed and supervised by a qualified BAMS/MD Ayurveda physician. Do not self-treat.

Digestive Disorders

Agnimandya (Weak Digestion / Dyspepsia)

Ayurvedic cause: Weakened Jatharagni. Herbs: Trikatu, Hingvastak churna, Chitrak, Pippali, Hing. Diet: Light, warm food; warm water with ginger and lemon; buttermilk. Treatment: Langhana (fasting/lightening), Deepana (Agni-kindling herbs), Pachana (Ama-digestive herbs).

Amlapitta (Hyperacidity / GERD)

Cause: Excess Pitta. Herbs: Shatavari, Yastimadhu (licorice), Amalaki, Shankha bhasma, Kamadudha rasa. Diet: Avoid spicy, sour, fried, fermented foods; cool sweet foods beneficial. Treatment: Virechana (purgation).

Grahani (IBS / Malabsorption Syndrome)

Cause: Chronic Agnimandya affecting Grahani (small intestine). Herbs: Kutaj (Holarrhena antidysenterica), Bilva, Musta, Nagarmotha, Shankha bhasma. Treatment: Basti, Deepana-Pachana therapy, Grahani Kapata rasa.

Musculoskeletal Disorders

Amavata (Rheumatoid Arthritis)

Cause: Ama + Vata in joints. Herbs: Guggulu (Simhanada, Yogaraj guggulu), Ashwagandha, Shallaki, Nirgundi, Rasna. Diet: Light, easily digestible food; ginger water; avoid curd, fish, heavy proteins. Treatment: Shodhana (purification) first, especially Virechana; then Shamana.

Sandhivata (Osteoarthritis)

Cause: Vata in joints with tissue depletion. Herbs: Ashwagandha, Shatavari, Bala, Mahanarayan taila. Treatment: Basti (primary), Abhyanga, Kati/Janu basti, Pinda sweda.

Gridhrasi (Sciatica)

Cause: Vata (and sometimes Kapha) affecting the sciatic nerve. Herbs: Dashamoola, Nirgundi, Shallaki, Mahayogaraj guggulu. Treatment: Basti (most effective), Kati basti, Pizhichil.

Skin Disorders

Kushtha (Skin diseases — broad category)

Cause: Imbalance of all three Doshas with predominant Pitta and Kapha; Ama in Rakta (blood). Herbs: Neem, Manjistha, Khadira, Haridra, Guduchi, Sariva, Triphala. Treatment: Virechana (primary), blood purification (Raktashodhana), Panchatikta ghrita internally.

Psoriasis (Ekakushtha/Kitibha)

Treatment: Extended Panchakarma (Virechana + Vamana), Panchatikta ghrita for 3-6 months, external application of Mahatiktak ghrita, Neem oil. Avoid dairy, red meat, fermented foods, alcohol.

Acne (Yuvana Pidika)

Cause: Excess Pitta + Kapha in Rakta (blood). Herbs: Manjistha, Neem, Guduchi, Turmeric, Sariva. Diet: Avoid spicy, oily, sugary foods. Regular Virechana in season.

Mental & Nervous System

Chittodvega (Anxiety)

Cause: Excess Vata in manovaha srotas (mind channels). Herbs: Brahmi, Shankhpushpi, Ashwagandha, Jatamansi, Vacha. Treatment: Shirodhara (primary), Nasya, Basti; daily Abhyanga; regular routine; Sattvic diet.

Nidranasha (Insomnia)

Cause: Vata/Pitta aggravation. Herbs: Ashwagandha, Tagara (Valeriana), Jatamansi, Brahmi. Treatment: Shirodhara, Abhyanga before sleep, warm milk with Ashwagandha and nutmeg, foot massage with sesame oil.

Avaroha Manasika (Depression)

Cause: Tama and Rajas imbalance, depleted Ojas, Sattva decline. Herbs: Brahmi, Ashwagandha, Shatavari, Shankhpushpi, Bhrami. Treatment: Nasya, Shirobasti, Shirodhara, Rasayana, yoga, pranayama, connection with nature and Sattva-promoting activities.

Yoga & Pranayama

Yoga & Pranayama in Ayurveda

Yoga and Ayurveda are sister sciences — both branches of the Vedic tree of knowledge. Patanjali's Yoga Sutras describe yoga as "Chitta Vritti Nirodha" — the cessation of fluctuations of the mind. Ayurveda provides the physical and physiological foundation; Yoga provides the mental and spiritual.

Yoga by Dosha Type

Vata Types — Grounding, Gentle, Warming

  • Hold poses longer; move slowly and mindfully
  • Recommended: Standing poses (Tadasana, Virabhadrasana), forward folds, inversions, Yin yoga, restorative yoga
  • Pranayama: Nadi Shodhana (alternate nostril), Bhramari, deep slow breathing
  • Avoid: Hot yoga, vigorous flow, rapid sequences

Pitta Types — Cooling, Surrendering, Compassionate

  • Practice with an attitude of non-competition; avoid pushing to extremes
  • Recommended: Moon salutations, cooling forward folds, twists, Yin yoga, nature-based practice
  • Pranayama: Sitali (cooling breath), Sitkari, Chandra Bhedana, Nadi Shodhana
  • Avoid: Hot yoga, intense competitive practice, power yoga in heat

Kapha Types — Stimulating, Energising, Warming

  • Practice vigorously; minimal rest between poses; stimulate and invigorate
  • Recommended: Sun salutations (multiple rounds), backbends, inversions, Vinyasa flow, Ashtanga
  • Pranayama: Kapalabhati (skull-shining breath), Bhastrika (bellows breath), Surya Bhedana
  • Avoid: Excessive Shavasana, very slow sedentary practice, Yin yoga as primary practice

Pranayama — The Science of Breath

Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril Breathing)

Balances left and right hemispheres, calms Vata, reduces anxiety, purifies energy channels (nadis). Practice: 5-15 minutes daily. Inhale left nostril, exhale right; inhale right, exhale left = 1 round.

Kapalabhati (Skull-Shining Breath)

Forceful exhalations with passive inhalations. Clears lungs, stimulates Agni, burns Ama, invigorates Kapha. 3 rounds of 30-100 repetitions. CONTRAINDICATED: Pregnancy, hypertension, heart conditions, hernia, epilepsy.

Bhramari (Bee Breath)

Humming on exhalation. Reduces Pitta and Vata, calms anxiety, improves sleep, beneficial for thyroid. Practice: 5-10 rounds. Particularly effective after Abhyanga and before Shirodhara.

Bhastrika (Bellows Breath)

Rapid forceful inhalations and exhalations. Stimulates Agni strongly, reduces Kapha, warms the body. 3 rounds of 10-20 rapid breaths. CONTRAINDICATED: Same as Kapalabhati.

Ujjayi (Victorious Breath)

Slight constriction of the glottis creating an oceanic sound. Used throughout Vinyasa yoga. Calms the nervous system, focuses the mind, creates internal heat. Good for all types.

Surya Namaskar — Sun Salutation

The complete moving meditation. 12 positions corresponding to the 12 signs of the zodiac. Done at sunrise facing East. A complete workout — stretches and strengthens all muscles, improves all physiological systems. Traditional texts recommend 108 rounds daily for maximum benefit. Begin with 3-5 rounds and gradually increase.

  • Pranamasana — Prayer pose; establishes intention
  • Hasta Uttanasana — Raised arms; stretches abdominals
  • Pada Hastasana — Forward fold; stretches posterior chain
  • Ashwa Sanchalanasana — Equestrian pose; opens hip flexors
  • Dandasana — Plank; core strength
  • Ashtanga Namaskara — Eight limbs; strengthens upper body
  • Bhujangasana — Cobra; opens chest, strengthens spine
  • Parvatasana — Mountain (downward dog); full body stretch
  • Ashwa Sanchalanasana — Equestrian; other leg
  • Pada Hastasana — Forward fold again
  • Hasta Uttanasana — Raised arms
  • Pranamasana — Return to prayer
